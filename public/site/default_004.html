Specialty Needs?


Cremation is an alternative method of final disposition to the traditional burial and families who choose cremation often have a wide variety of preferences with regards to the type of casket their loved one would be cremated in. Our selection offers a wide range of caskets from a simple box to caskets that look similar to the traditional burial casket.
For those who follow the traditional Jewish beliefs, a casket must be made entirely of wood. The use of metal such as in nails, screws, handles, etc. is forbidden as it is not in keeping with Jewish law. Orthodox Jewish caskets are constructed entirely of wood using dowels and glue instead of screws and nails.
As the general population grows in size, we have committed to providing a product line that provides an ample assortment of enhanced width caskets with regards to the varieties of colors and themes often found in standard width caskets.
For those unfortunate circumstances when the life of a youth is taken before its years, we offer a variety of child funeral products to commemorate the life that was lived. The youth product line offers caskets that will suit both the traditional burial and cremation preferences of the family.

Type of Metal

Metal caskets are constructed multiple types of metals including Bronze, Copper, Stainless Steel and Carbon Steel. Carbon steel has several thicknesses available including 16 Gauge, 18 Gauge and 20 Gauge steel with 16 Gauge being the most thick and 20 Gauge being the thinnest.
